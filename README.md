# HEULAULAB — GUIDEBOOK

**Version 1.0 — Source of Truth**

---

## 0. Premise

HEULAULAB is not a service.
It is a point of view.

If the work does not reflect that point of view, it does not belong here.

---

## 1. Core Thesis

**Most brands fail because their product, identity, and space are designed as separate surfaces instead of one behavioral system.**

Everything we do must prove this wrong.

---

## 2. What HEULAULAB Actually Is

A multidisciplinary design lab working across:

* Software (interfaces, systems)
* Brand (identity, communication)
* Space (interior, architecture)

Not as separate services —
but as one **integrated behavioral system**.

---

## 3. What We Believe

### 3.1 Design is not decoration

If it doesn’t change behavior, it’s not design.

### 3.2 Systems over surfaces

A logo, a UI, a room — none matter alone.

### 3.3 Clarity over comfort

If it feels too safe, it’s probably irrelevant.

### 3.4 Not for everyone

Relevance > approval.

---

## 4. What We Reject

* Generic “clean modern” design
* Template-driven thinking
* Over-explained UX
* Decorative branding
* Safe, consensus-based decisions

If everyone agrees immediately, it’s probably weak.

---

## 5. Audience

**Primary:**
Cross-disciplinary teams (dev + designer + strategist) building products and spaces — but failing to connect them.

**We speak to:**

* Founders with intent
* Teams with friction
* People tired of surface-level design

**We do NOT target:**

* Safe corporate decision-makers
* Clients who want “just a website”
* Anyone optimizing for approval

---

## 6. Positioning

**A design lab for brands that don’t want to look normal.**

Alternative:

* We design things people don’t ignore.
* Not for everyone. That’s the point.

---

## 7. Tone of Voice

### Core Traits

* Direct
* Opinionated
* Controlled, not loud
* Confident, not friendly

### Rules

* No buzzwords
* No corporate language
* No filler
* Every sentence must carry weight

### Example

❌ “We help brands grow with innovative solutions”
✅ “If your brand looks like everyone else, we’re not interested.”

---

## 8. Visual System

### 8.1 Foundation — Brutal Modernism (80%)

* Strong grid
* High contrast (black / white)
* Large, dominant typography
* Asymmetry allowed (intentional)

### 8.2 Accent — Mid-Century Warmth (20%)

* Burnt orange / olive / muted yellow
* Subtle curves
* Human touch

### Rule:

If it starts to feel “comfortable”, pull it back.

---

## 9. Typography

* Primary: grotesk / neo-grotesk (bold, tight)
* Secondary: serif (for contrast, minimal use)

Usage:

* Headlines = aggressive
* Body = controlled

---

## 10. Layout Principles

* Grid first, break second
* Whitespace is structure, not decoration
* Kill unnecessary elements
* If unsure → remove, not add

---

## 11. Interaction Principles

* Minimal animation
* Slight friction is acceptable
* No “buttery smooth for the sake of it”

---

## 12. Project Selection Rules

### Accept if:

* There is room to influence system-level decisions
* The client values difference over safety

### Reject if:

* It’s purely execution (“just build this”)
* No control over direction
* Fully template-driven

---

## 13. Portfolio Rules

Not all work is shown.

Only include:

* Work that reflects system thinking
* Work that has a clear narrative

Exclude:

* Generic execution
* Compromised projects

---

## 14. Case Study Structure

Every project must explain:

1. Problem (real, not cosmetic)
2. System (how things connect)
3. Output (visual / spatial / digital)
4. Impact (behavior change)

If one is missing, it’s incomplete.

---

## 15. Content Strategy

Content is not marketing.
It is positioning.

### Core Narrative:

Design today is surface-level. We build systems.

### Content Rules:

* Opinion first, education second
* Challenge assumptions
* Repeat the thesis relentlessly

---

## 16. Internal Standard

Before shipping anything, ask:

* Does this look like everyone else?
* Does this change behavior?
* Does this reflect system thinking?
* Would we stand behind this publicly?

If any answer is “no” → redo.

---

## 17. Growth Strategy

We do not grow by volume.
We grow by signal.

* Fewer projects
* Stronger opinions
* Clearer identity

---

## 18. Non-Negotiables

* No generic work under HEULAULAB
* No diluted messaging
* No trend-chasing

Break these, and the brand collapses.

---

## 19. Final Principle

We are not here to make things look better.

We are here to make things **work as systems**.

Everything else is noise.
